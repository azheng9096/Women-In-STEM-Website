Gender Gap in STEM (Census)

The percentage of women in the STEM workforce increased from 8% in 1970 to 27% in 2019. However, despite the progress made, we still see a large gender gap in the STEM workforce.

Percentage of Women in STEM Jobs [1970 to 2019] (Census)

Despite women holding the more social scientist positions than men, and statistically indifferent percentage of mathematical worker and life and physical scientists positions than men, women make up a significantly lower percentage of the workforce in engineering and computer occupations, which “made up the largest portion (80%) of the STEM workforce” (Census)
Image Source: Census.gov

Percentage of Women with STEM Degrees [2017-2018] and in STEM Jobs [2019] (Catalyst)

Overall, women have a small share of STEM degrees (30%-40% across all degree levels) (Catalyst). Similar pattern/percentage of women with degrees in specific STEM field compared to percentage of women with specific STEM jobs. Again, women hold a significantly lower percentage of the degrees in engineering and computer field.

Women of Color in STEM (Catalyst)

Women of color earned a mere 14.1% of STEM bachelor's degree in 2017-2018, and only 11.5% of women held positions in science and engineering (Catalyst). The chart shows a portion of these percentages.
It is also worth noting that, according to Catalyst, "Women of Color Earn the Smallest Share of STEM Degrees".

Earnings

Different STEM Occupation Median Earning Gap Based on Gender (Census)

Most STEM occupations have statistically different earnings between men and women, with earnings for women on the lower side. Even STEM occupations with earnings between men and women that are not statistically different (grey), data in the image shows the earnings for women are overall lower than men.
Image Source: Census.gov

Earnings of Women and Men in STEM By Racial and Ethnic Groups (Pew Research Center)

There is a pay gap between women of different races. While STEM jobs still, in general, pay higher than jobs in other fields, research shows that Asian women earn more than Black women and Hispanic women. The median annual earnings of Asian women across all STEM jobs are $88,600, while it is $57,000 for Black women and Hispanic women.
Overall, as per the chart heading, "Across all racial and ethnic groups, women in STEM earn less than their male counterparts."
Image Source: Pew Research Center
