Passion Infects

If you are a female college student or female working adult who is passionate about the STEM-field career you are pursuing or are in, try discussing with your local elementary, middle, or high school to hold a guest speaking (or similar) event to expose students to STEM. You just may be the role model female students need to motivate them into giving STEM a try.


Women in School Curriculum

In history classes, rarely are women in STEM figures (or their contributions) discussed and remembered. Discuss with your local schools to to include/increase the discussion of successful women in STEM figures in their history/social science curriculum. Young girls may find a role model (who are like them) to serve as a motivator, who otherwise may remain unknown to them. Young boys would also become aware that women are capable of pursuing a career in the STEM field. This would reduce discrimination against women in STEM as it cultivates the younger generation to reject the discriminatory mindset of gender determining a person's qualification in STEM.
